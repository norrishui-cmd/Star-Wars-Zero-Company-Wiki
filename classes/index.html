All 8 Standard Specializations

Compare the eight roles officially named by EA and see what remains unknown before launch.

Officially confirmedChecked July 18, 2026

Quick Answer

EA has confirmed eight standard Specializations: Assault, Gunslinger, Heavy, Medic, Scoundrel, Scout, Sharpshooter and Soldier. Most Operators use one of these roles, and most can change Specialization through the Personnel facility.

Eight Standard Specializations

Assault
Frontline fighter: Maneuvers aggressively to defeat targets hiding in cover.Gunslinger
Multi-target attacker: Uses multi-target blaster attacks and fast strikes to spread offense across several targets.Heavy
Durable frontline combatant: Uses armor and survivability to absorb pressure while fighting in the open.Medic
Healing and Injury prevention: Heals Operators, helps prevent Injuries and can boost teammates physically and psychologically.Scoundrel
Opportunistic wildcard: Takes advantage of messy situations with little preparation and makes the most of combat opportunities.Scout
Reconnaissance and setup: Uses planning and timing to create opportunities for the whole squad.Sharpshooter
Long-range marksman: Singles out chosen enemies and excels in long-range engagements.Soldier
Mainstay offensive unit: Fights with melee, grenades, heavy blaster fire and a powerful rocket launcher.

Why There Is No Pre-launch Tier List

A trustworthy tier list requires final Ability text, AP costs, enemy design, mission objectives, difficulty settings and launch balance. Those inputs are not fully public. This hub therefore compares official roles without assigning S, A or B rankings.

Shared Structure

Every standard Specialization has three unique Ability components: an Ultimate, a Standard Ability and a Passive.

Progression

Focus Points can improve Talents, Abilities and Passives. Bonds and Cross Training can also add permanent combat bonuses to Operator pairs.
